Dennis Ritchie

life and greatness of a computer scientist

One of Dennis Ritchie's famous statements: UNIX is Simple

Those are some of the most important moments of his life:

  • 1941 - Born in Bronxville, NY
  • 1963 - Obtained his bachelor's degree in Physics from Harvard University
  • 1967 - Started working at Bell Labs, working in the operating system Multics
  • 1968 - Obtained his Doctorate's degree in Mathematics also from Harvard
  • 1969 - Multics dropped the development of Multics, so Ritchie is prompted to develop C and started to work on UNIX
  • 1971 - Unix was released working in a variety of platforms
  • 1972 - C programming language
  • 1973 - Along Thompson they rewrote UNIX in the C language
  • 1983 - Received the A.M. Turing award, the top achievement in Computer Science, for: "their development of generic operating systems theory and specifically for the implementation of the UNIX operating system" (Hosch).
  • 1988 - Elected into the U.S. National Academy of Engineering
  • 1990 - Appointed the head of the System Software Research at Bell Labs
  • 1994 - Winner of the Computer Pioneer Award
  • 1999 - U.S National Medal of Technology Award
  • 2011 - Death
"Las herramientas que Dennis construyó - Y sus descendientes directos - hacen funcionar practicamente todo hoy en día."
- Brian Kernighan for New York Times after Dennis's Dead
